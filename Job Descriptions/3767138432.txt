        Position Summary<br/><br/>The Board of Bar Overseers of the Supreme Judicial Court (BBO) seeks an experienced full-time Director of Technology. The Director manages a team of IT professionals that supports functions across the Board of Bar Overseers, including Attorney Registration, the Office of General Counsel, the Office of Bar Counsel, the Clients’ Security Board, and Administration. The BBO has about ninety full-time members who work in a hybrid environment, with its office currently located at 99 High Street, Boston, MA. This mission-driven organization relies on an integrated Salesforce and Microsoft technology solution that requires continual maintenance and development to ensure the organization can effectively and efficiently fulfill its mission. The Director will lead the development of the IT strategy, provide planning and management of all operational activities of the IT Department, and will serve as an essential member of the leadership team. <br/><br/>The Director of Information Technology’ primary responsibilities include:<br/><br/><ul><li>Consults with senior-level stakeholders across the organizational departments to identify technology needs presented by the user communities, determines where efficiencies and/or automation can occur, educates users, and recommends technical solutions, providing project planning and implementation for success.</li><li>Responsible for management and success of IT team members, including hiring, training, evaluating, and mentoring to develop talent and make sure the team is meeting the needs and goals of the business.</li><li>Develops and implements business continuity protocols to minimize disruption to business operations in an emergency or data loss.</li><li>Continually assess and improve IT infrastructure and systems performance to assess operating costs, productivity levels, and upgrade requirements.</li><li>Ensures excellent service for internal and external users of the IT system.</li><li>Recommends and develops plans for systems development and operations, hardware and software purchases, and staffing.</li><li>Will help develop a budget plan for the IT department.</li><li>Will negotiate and manage contracts with consultants, technical personnel and vendors for services and products.</li><li>Ensures that technical resources are available to promptly support and assist employees with help desk and other technical requests, including providing emergency responses as needed.</li><li>Maintains all IT assets and asset inventory and verifies compliance.</li><li>Supervises all additional essential technology including phone systems, fax systems, printers, physical security systems, media, audio-visual, etc.</li><li>Arranges for or provides appropriate training and develops appropriate support documentation for employees' use of technology.<br/><br/></li></ul>All candidates will demonstrate excellent communications skills, a proven ability to manage an IT department and work as part of a collaborative leadership team focused on ensuring the organization can fulfill its mission. At a minimum, candidates will have a bachelor’s degree in computer science or a<br/><br/>related field, but a master’s degree is preferred. Candidates will also have experience with Microsoft technologies and cloud platforms, Salesforce, and document management systems. Candidates will have at least ten years of information technology experience and six years of supervisory experience. Candidates will also have experience with systems infrastructure operations, networking, security, and end-user support. In addition, candidates will maintain commitments to the highest ethical and professional standards, to advancing racial justice, and to improving the bar's quality.<br/><br/>Salary and Benefits<br/><br/>Excellent benefits and a salary commensurate with experience. The salary range for this position is $85,540 to $143,314. New employees typically begin their employment near the minimum to mid-point of the range depending on the prior experience, education, training and/or skill set being brought to the position.<br/><br/>The BBO offers flexibility in individual workplace arrangements providing opportunities for regular alternative work and remote work schedules.<br/><br/>Applications and Inquiries<br/><br/>At the BBO, we embrace diversity, and we are seeking to nurture our DEI efforts so diversity will thrive because it benefits our organization, the bar, and the public we serve. In addition to federal law requirements, the BBO complies with applicable state and local laws governing nondiscrimination in employment.<br/><br/>The BBO actively encourages applications from candidates, including those from marginalized communities, who would both contribute to and learn from working in a diverse, equitable, and inclusive workplace.
