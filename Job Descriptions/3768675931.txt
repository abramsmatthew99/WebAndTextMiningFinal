<p><strong>POSITION TITLE:</strong> Business Analyst</p><p><strong>DEPARTMENT: </strong>Transformation Office</p><p><br/></p><p><strong><em>Ora values the daily practice of … </em></strong></p><p><em>Prioritizing Kindness * Operational Excellence * Cultivating Joy * Scientific Rigor</em></p><p><strong>_______________________________________________________________</strong></p><p><br/></p><p><strong>The Role:</strong></p><p>The Business Analyst is a new role supporting our newly created Project Management Office at Ora. The Business Analyst will be responsible for collecting and analyzing data, generating insights, and providing critical information to aid in the decision-making supporting the continuous improvement driving PMO initiatives within Ora (clinical operations, education/performance improvement, project management, amongst others.) This role will support efforts ensuring alignment between project management and continuous improvement efforts. This role is remote and will report to the VP, Transformation.</p><p><br/></p><p><strong>What You’ll Do:</strong></p><ul><li>Provide analysis expertise to the development of project planning and project performance evaluation measures set up to various divisions across the enterprise.</li><li>Provide concise reports on project performance to support determining and optimizing resource allocation.</li><li>Identify possible issues with project reporting/data gathering with the goal of developing strategies to continuously improve processes and procedures.</li><li>Partner with various departments to lead requirements gathering, target setting, reports/dashboards design, process documentation, and provide analytical support as required, ensuring alignment with divisional and project goals and objectives.</li><li>Use analytical models and statistical methods to review the viability of proposed initiatives and assess the impacts of current trends/practices on existing processes.</li><li>Present recommendations to management to support fact-based decision-making, ensuring alignment with stakeholder requirements, company policies, standards, and regulatory requirements. </li></ul><p><br/></p><p><strong>What We Look For:</strong></p><p><br/></p><p><em>Experience needed for the Role:</em></p><ul><li>Bachelor's degree with an emphasis in Business or Computer Science field, preferred.</li><li>2+ years of previous experience in Analysis &amp; Reporting, technical experience on designing, implementing and maintaining data/reports in a business intelligence and project management environment.</li><li>Strong understanding of concepts, principles, practices, and techniques pertaining to all aspects of performance measures, benchmarking, balanced scorecards, and dashboards.</li><li>Strong understanding of PowerBI and Macros (VBA), and other business Intelligence tools.</li><li>Strong knowledge and understanding of the relevant concepts of data mining and data warehousing to be able to procure and manage data.</li><li>Knowledge in research, policy writing, and project management fundamentals.</li><li>Excellent knowledge of office software including word processing, spreadsheet, and data management systems.</li></ul><p><br/></p><p><em>Additional Skills &amp; Attributes</em></p><ul><li>Strong analytical, problem-solving, critical thinking, and judgment skills to be able to interpret data and identify, diagnose, and resolve issues.</li><li>Strong relationship-building skills and ability to work across multidisciplinary teams and management levels.</li><li>Strong time management, organization, verbal, and written communication skills.</li></ul><p><br/></p><p><em>Competencies and Personal Traits:</em></p><ul><li>Attention to Detail: Ability to analyze data to drive successful solutions to complex problems.</li><li>Emotionally Intelligent Leadership: Ability to lead with a positive mindset and empathy.</li><li>Intellectual Curiosity: Willingness and desire to learn new things and dig deeper than the surface.</li><li>Innovative Mindset: Forward thinking, creative, and open to testing, making mistakes, and trying again.</li><li>Organization and Goal Setting: Ability to set goals, develop structure, and maintain a focused approach to critical path work.</li><li>Resolve Conflicts: Practice radical candor in your communication and participate in active listening to help the other person feel heard and understood.</li><li>Seek Meaning and Purpose and a Desire to be Part of Something Bigger than Yourself: Crave being part of a team that works together under shared values to achieve significant positive impact in the wider world. </li></ul><p><br/></p><p><strong>What We Offer:</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Well-Being: </strong>Offering comprehensive healthcare options in Medical, Dental, and Vision beginning day 1.</li><li><strong>Flexible PTO &amp; Unlimited Sick Time: </strong>Providing you the freedom to unwind and recharge when you need to in addition to 14 company paid holidays.</li><li><strong>Financial</strong>: Competitive salaries along with a 401K plan through Fidelity with company match.</li><li><strong>Family Support Care: </strong>Adoption and fertility assistance provided along with offering 16 weeks paid Parental Leave.</li><li><strong>Company Paid Life &amp; Disability Insurance: </strong>Offering peace of mind to help you and your family feel secure.</li><li><strong>Remote &amp; Wellness Reimbursement: </strong>We'll reimburse you to support your remote workspace and wellness purchases.</li><li><strong>Employee Assistance Program: </strong>No matter what issues you're facing, New Directions is here to help you and your family.</li><li><strong>Career Development Opportunities</strong>: Continued opportunities to grow and develop your career journey.</li><li><strong>Global Team: </strong>Opportunities to work with colleagues across the globe.</li><li><strong>Impact</strong>: A chance to research new ophthalmic therapies that will impact patients across the globe. </li></ul><p><br/></p><p><strong>Benefit Eligibility: </strong>Full-time employees of Ora working a minimum of 30 hours per week. Our per diem workforce is eligible for Medical and Dental coverage once they have hit 6 months of service and work an average of 30 hours per week.</p><p><br/></p><p>Our mission is to weave together people, processes, and technology to support innovation in ophthalmology around the world. We believe our business should be a force for good — to improve, heal, and change how we see. As a global company, our vibrant community and culture are nurtured by our core values: Prioritizing kindness, cultivating joy, operational excellence, and scientific rigor. Through our commitment to these values, we have built an inclusive and supportive work environment that fosters respect, accountability, and a fulfilling work-life balance for every team member.</p><p><br/></p><p>We know a diverse workforce adds to our collective value and strength as an organization.
