        This position is with the Office of Information and Technology, within U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP), with the Field Support Directorate office located in Bloomington, MN.<br/><br/>If selected, you will become a key team member of Homeland Security professionals to perform technical and analytical work pertaining to acquisition, testing, integration, installation, utilization, maintenance or modification of IT systems, facilities, services, and procedures. Joining CBP will allow you to use your expertise in Information Technology principles, concepts, and methods to support Information Technology systems and services.<br/><br/><strong>Major Duties Include<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Diagnosing and resolving problems in response to customer reported incidents.</li><li>Researching, evaluating, and providing feedback on problematic trends and patterns to support customer support requirements.</li><li>Interacting with internal and external stakeholders as the Subject Matter Expert to provide IT guidance and solutions and providing customer training as required.</li><li>Installing, configuring, integrating, troubleshooting, optimizing, and maintaining customer hardware and software to maximize system availability and monitors network performance.</li><li>Identifying and specifying information system security requirements and ensuring application of information security/information assurance policies, principles, and practices. <br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Salary And Benefits<br/><br/></strong>This position starts at a salary of $74,973.00 (GS-11, Step 1) to $116,821.00 (GS-12, Step 10) with promotion potential to $116,821.00 (GS-12, Step 10). Employees receive the full package of federal employment benefits that include health and insurance plans, a generous annual and sick leave program, and participation in the Thrift Savings Plan, a retirement plan akin to a traditional 401(k) offering.<br/><br/><strong>Qualifications<br/><br/></strong>This position will be filled at either the GS-11 or GS-12 grade level, as described below and depending upon the qualifications of each applicant.<br/><br/><strong>Note: There is both a basic experience requirement and an education substitution (see below) for experience that is allowed for the GS-11 grade level.<br/><br/></strong><strong>Basic Experience Requirement:</strong> Applicants for BOTH the GS-11 and GS-12 grade levels must have IT-related experience demonstrating each of these four competencies:<br/><br/><ul><li>Attention to Detail - Is thorough when performing work and conscientious about attending to detail.</li><li>Customer Service - Works with clients and customers (that is, any individuals who use or receive the services or products that your work unit produces, including the general public, individuals who work in the agency, other agencies, or organizations outside the Government) to assess their needs, provide information or assistance, resolve their problems, or satisfy their expectations; knows about available products and services; is committed to providing quality products and services.</li><li>Oral Communication - Expresses information (for example, ideas or facts) to individuals or groups effectively, considering the audience and nature of the information (for example, technical, sensitive, controversial); makes clear and convincing oral presentations; listens to others, attends to nonverbal cues, and responds appropriately.</li><li>Problem Solving - Identifies problems; determines accuracy and relevance of information; uses sound judgment to generate and evaluate alternatives, and to make recommendations.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>OR Education Substitution-GS-11 only:</strong> Successful completion of a Ph.D. or equivalent doctoral degree or three (3) full years of progressively higher-level graduate education leading to a Ph.D. or equivalent doctoral degree in an accredited college or university. A degree in computer science, engineering, information science, information systems management, mathematics, operations research, statistics, or technology management <strong>or</strong> degree that provided a minimum of 24 semester hours in one or more of the fields identified above and required the development or adaptation of applications, systems, or networks.<br/><br/><strong>Specialized Experience: </strong>You qualify for the GS-11 grade level if you meet the basic requirement above and possess one year of specialized experience equivalent to at least the next lower grade level, performing duties such as:<br/><br/><ul><li>Analyzing, planning, integrating, and configuring network systems hardware.</li><li>Diagnosing and resolving network problems, hardware/software interface and interoperability problems.</li><li>Testing, installing, configuring, and maintaining networks including hardware, middleware, and software to ensure conformance to national standards consulting.</li><li>Evaluating new hardware and software technologies for potential inclusion into the enterprise.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Duties<br/><br/></strong>You qualify for the GS-12 grade level if you meet the basic requirement above and possess one (1) year of specialized experience equivalent to at least the next lower grade level, performing duties such as:<br/><br/><ul><li>Adjusting, modifying, and improving Information Technology systems.</li><li>Diagnosing and resolving problems in response to customer reported incidents involving different platforms, operating systems, applications, and desktop configurations.</li><li>Installing, configuring, integrating, troubleshooting, optimizing, and monitoring network performance.</li><li>Providing guidance and solutions in planning and implementing migration activities to ensure successful deployment of technologies.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>NOTE: Your resume must explicitly indicate how you meet these requirements, otherwise you will be found ineligible. Be sure to review the entirety of the official </strong><strong>Job Opportunity Announcement (JOA) on USAJOBS.gov, the federal government's official employment site.<br/><br/></strong><strong>How To Apply<br/><br/></strong>Click this site's APPLY link to access the official JOA on USAJOBS.gov, the federal government's official employment site. You will learn exactly how to submit your application package, including your resume, which must explicitly indicate how you meet the above Specialized Experience and/or Education Requirements, otherwise you will be found ineligible.<br/><br/><strong>The closing date for this employment opportunity is December 13, 2023.<br/><br/></strong>Interested in other career opportunities with CBP? Visit <strong>www.cbp.gov/careers</strong> and check the CBP calendar at <strong>www.cbp.gov/cbp-events-calendar</strong> frequently for upcoming informational webinars, in-person recruiter events, and virtual career events.
