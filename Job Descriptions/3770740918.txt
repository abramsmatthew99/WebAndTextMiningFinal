<strong>Description<br/><br/></strong>Under general supervision, to identify potential privacy weaknesses and vulnerabilities and recommend privacy controls to mitigate vulnerabilities and reduce overall privacy risks; and to assess privacy policies, procedures, and operations to help ensure the organization meets privacy requirements and government regulations for the protection of personal and sensitive information.<br/><br/><strong>This Privacy Analyst position is an information privacy role supporting the County Privacy Officer in developing and implementing privacy controls, training, and awareness. The Privacy Analyst will draw upon past compliance or privacy program experience to assist County agency contacts with improving privacy controls, conducting assessments, promoting privacy-by-design in project plans, software/product development life cycles, and upgrades.<br/><br/></strong><strong>This position is required to be on-site each working day at the County Executive Office in San Jose, CA during the nine-month probationary period</strong><strong>. A hybrid schedule may be allowed contingent upon successful probation completion based on the work-from-home program in effect at that time. However, this position may be required to report to the office on scheduled work-from-home days as required by the department executive.<br/><br/></strong><strong> COVID-19 Risk Tier – Low Risk <br/><br/></strong>Typical Tasks<br/><br/>The following are the duties performed by employees in this classification. However, employees may perform other related duties at an equivalent level. Each individual in the classification does not necessarily perform all duties listed.<br/><br/><ul><li>Contributes to the review, implementation, and updates of Countywide privacy policies, standards, procedures, and controls;</li><li>Reviews information systems designs to adequately incorporate privacy controls around choice, consent, collection, notice, use, retention, disposal, and third-party disclosures, where applicable;</li><li>Identifies and investigates privacy incidents that violate the County’s privacy policies;</li><li>Investigates privacy complaints and adopts the appropriate standardized steps to respond to and address complaints;</li><li>Identifies, develops, and aligns techniques to aggregate, anonymize, or de-identify data;</li><li>Identifies information assets and classifies them based on their level of sensitivity, value, and criticality to the organization;</li><li>Assists with risk analyses of County privacy projects and capabilities;</li><li>Completes Sensitive Information Inventories (SIIs), Privacy Threshold Analyses (PTAs), and Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs), and creates reports, charts, and other material, as needed, to demonstrate findings;</li><li>Assists in the development and review of privacy training material and serves as a privacy training instructor to educate and update employees on privacy requirements, issues, and best practices;</li><li>Facilitates training, awareness, and communication of privacy matters, including compliance requirements, across the County;</li><li>Maintains an awareness of and reports on applicable Federal and State privacy laws and standards, as well as monitors advancements in information privacy technologies;</li><li>May be assigned as a Disaster Service Worker (DSW), as required*;</li><li>Performs other related duties, as required.<br/><br/></li></ul>Employment Standards<br/><br/><strong>Education<br/><br/></strong>Sufficient education, training, and experience to demonstrate the ability to perform the above tasks, plus the possession and direct application of the following knowledge and abilities:<br/><br/><strong>Training and Experience Note:</strong> The required knowledge and abilities are attained through training and experience equivalent to a Bachelor’s degree, preferably in Data Communications, Computer Science, Information Systems, Network Engineering, Information Privacy, Privacy Law, or a closely related field. Relevant analytical, administrative, lead, supervisory, or management experience that includes interpreting rules and regulations, gathering data and formulating recommendations, and report writing can substitute for education on a year-for-year basis,<br/><br/>and<br/><br/>Four (4) years of professional level experience in the privacy, legal, technology, compliance, or information security fields, one (1) year of which must have been working with medium to large scale information privacy or security projects.<br/><br/><strong>Special Requirements<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Ability to travel to alternate locations in the course of work. If driving, possession of a valid California driver’s license prior to appointment and the ability to qualify for and maintain a County driver authorization.</li><li>*As a condition of employment, pursuant to California Government Code Sections 3100-3109 and Local Ordinance, all County of Santa Clara employees are designated Disaster Service Workers, including extra help. A DSW is required to complete all assigned DSW-related training, and in the event of an emergency, return to work as ordered.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Knowledge Of<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Applicable security policies and practices;</li><li>Personally Identifiable Information (PII) inventory, information classification, and privacy threat modeling;</li><li>Principles of effective communication;</li><li>Incident response process from a privacy context;</li><li>Privacy and data security laws and issues;</li><li>Basic principles of curriculum development and training techniques;</li><li>Information privacy or security forensic tools;</li><li>Privacy principles, Privacy-by-Design, phases of the Software Development Life Cycle, and related terminology, trends, and activities utilized by medium to large complex organizations;</li><li>Privacy impact assessment (PIA) methodology, including interviewing and identifying risks;</li><li>Methods of research and report preparation, writing, and presentation;</li><li>English usage, style, grammar, punctuation, and spelling;</li><li>Current privacy technologies for data mapping;</li><li>Metrics that demonstrate information security control effectiveness;</li><li>Types and applications of data de-identification.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Ability To<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Consult on privacy, security, and compliance;</li><li>Lead interview sessions, PIA activities, and recommend/oversee risk remediation activities and solutions that provide the proper level of privacy protection over personal and sensitive information;</li><li>Troubleshoot privacy and data protection problems and identify and recommend alternative solutions;</li><li>Recommend corrective actions to comply with Federal and State regulations and County policies;</li><li>Perform privacy impact assessments and recommend solutions that provide the proper level of privacy protection over personal and sensitive information;</li><li>Apply information privacy principles to business processes and information systems;</li><li>Communicate effectively both orally and in writing, with people of diverse backgrounds and cultures;</li><li>Establish and maintain effective working relationships with those contacted in the course of work, at all levels, including colleagues, the public, and representatives of other agencies;</li><li>Monitor compliance with privacy policies, standards, guidelines, and procedures;</li><li>Prepare detailed technical reports and other documentation;</li><li>Work effectively with other staff;</li><li>Conduct privacy audits.<br/><br/></li></ul>Physical Requirements<br/><br/><ul><li>May be required to work irregular hours.</li></ul>
