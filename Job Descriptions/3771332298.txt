<strong>Job Details<br/><br/></strong><strong>Description<br/><br/></strong><strong>POSITION CODE: </strong>6945<br/><br/><strong>DEPARTMENT/ADMINISTRATION: </strong>Information Technology Services<br/><br/><strong>APPOINTMENT: </strong>Administrative, Full-time (40 hours), Twelve (12) Months<br/><br/><strong>SALARY RANGE: </strong>$67,700 - $77,500 Annually, Pro-rated<br/><br/><strong>HIRING RANGE: </strong>Anticipated hiring range is near or at the minimum of the classification, commensurate with candidate's education, experience, skills, and training.<br/><br/><strong>INTRODUCTORY PERIOD: </strong>Twelve (12) Months<br/><br/><strong>AVAILABLE: </strong>Immediately<br/><br/><strong>POSTING DATE: </strong>November 21, 2023<br/><br/><strong>APPLICATION DEADLINE:</strong> Open Until Filled. First review of applicants expected December 11, 2023.<br/><br/><strong>Definition Of Classification<br/><br/></strong>Reporting to the Chief Technology Officer, the Infrastructure Analyst (analyst) is vital to designing, implementing, and maintaining the university’s Information Technology (IT) infrastructure. The position works on a wide range of projects related to the network, systems, servers, storage, cloud environment and data centers. The position contributes to the development and enhancement of our technological infrastructure, ensuring its stability and reliability to support the university's academic and administrative needs.<br/><br/>The Analyst will also collaborate with other members of the Infrastructure team, Technology Support Services team, Business Analysts, Developers, and Instructional Technologists in the implementation, support, and enhancement of technologies that support the University’s faculty, staff, and students.<br/><br/>This position requires flexibility to work evening and weekend hours as well as the ability to travel, as required.<br/><br/><strong>Duties And Responsibilities<br/><br/></strong>The duties and responsibilities include, but are not limited to, the following:<br/><br/><strong>Infrastructure Design And Planning<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Collaborate with cross-functional teams to design and plan infrastructure solutions that meet the university's requirements. </li><li>Evaluate and recommend hardware, software, and network technologies to improve efficiency and scalability.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Infrastructure Implementation<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Configure, install, and maintain network equipment, servers, and storage systems.</li><li>Deploy and manage virtualization technologies, such as VMware or Hyper-V. <br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Network Management<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Monitor network performance and ensure high availability, security, and reliability. </li><li>Troubleshoot and resolve network-related issues in a timely manner. <br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Server Administration<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Administer Windows and Linux server environments.</li><li>Perform routine server maintenance, patch management, and upgrades. <br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Storage Management<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Manage and optimize storage systems, including SAN and NAS solutions. </li><li>Implement data backup and disaster recovery strategies. <br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Security And Compliance<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Implement security best practices to safeguard university data and infrastructure. </li><li>Ensure compliance with relevant industry standards and regulations. <br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Documentation And Reporting<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Maintain accurate documentation of infrastructure configurations and procedures. </li><li>Generate regular reports on infrastructure performance and capacity planning. <br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Collaboration And Support<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Collaborate with IT colleagues to provide technical support to university staff, faculty, and students. </li><li>Assist in resolving escalated technical issues. </li><li>Performs other related duties and special projects as assigned.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Qualification Guidelines<br/><br/></strong>Any combination equivalent to, but not limited to, the following:<br/><br/><strong>Experience/Training/Education<br/><br/></strong>Required<br/><br/><ul><li>Bachelor's degree in computer science, computer engineering, or related field, or an equivalent combination of education, training, and experience.</li><li>Three (3) years of recent experience with increasing responsibility and demonstrated expertise in information technology systems engineering focusing on end user computing solutions or related experience.</li><li>Must possess a valid driver’s license with a good driving Note: Out-of-state licenses are valid until appointment. A valid California Class “C” driver’s license must be obtained within ten (10) days of appointment (in accordance with CA Vehicle Code 12505c).<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Knowledge And Skills<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Knowledge of information technology infrastructure requirements of an organization. </li><li>Knowledge of networks in a complex enterprise environment.</li><li>Knowledge of Microsoft Windows, MacOS, and Linux operating systems. </li><li>Knowledge of PowerShell language and experience with Microsoft ecosystem. </li><li>Knowledge of the appropriate safety precautions and procedures. </li><li>Ability to translate administrative and academic needs into design and implementation of appropriate support programs. </li><li>Ability to resolve design, implementation, and operational problems effectively. </li><li>Ability to prepare and clearly present complex technological concepts and alternatives to all levels of staff.</li><li>Knowledge of applicable, federal, and state laws and regulations. </li><li>Ability to apply legal and policy standards. </li><li>Ability to facilitate cooperation through discussion and consensus-building. </li><li>Excellent oral and written communication skills. </li><li>Excellent ability to clearly communicate ideas and recommendations.</li><li>Strong organizational skills in handling, directing, and prioritizing multiple and complex assignments/projects simultaneously. </li><li>Ability to be creative and flexible. </li><li>Ability to work independently with little direction.</li><li>Creative problem solver. </li><li>Customer service oriented. </li><li>Strong interpersonal skills using tact, patience, and courtesy.</li><li>Effective problem-solving skills. </li><li>Ability to learn to interpret, apply and explain rules, regulations, policies, and procedures.</li><li>Proficient computer skills using Microsoft Office Suite and other applications. </li><li>Ability to maintain confidentiality and act with professionalism, sensitivity, and neutrality. </li><li>Ability to use appropriate judgment when making decisions and recommendations. </li><li>Must be able to competently interact with a culturally and ethnically diverse population of students, faculty, and staff.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Physical Requirements/Working Conditions<br/><br/></strong>Working Conditions:<br/><br/>Standard office and field setting. Duties performed are in the field and in an office environment while sitting at a desk or computer workstation or in meetings. At least minimal environmental controls to assure health and comfort.<br/><br/><strong>Physical Demands<br/><br/></strong>Incumbents regularly stand for long periods of time; speak clearly and distinctly to provide information; see to read fine print and operate computer; hear and understand voices over telephone and in person; use hands and fingers to operate an electronic keyboard or other office machines; walk on a regular basis; travel to various locations; finger dexterity may be required to utilize and operate the specialized tools and equipment; distinguish colors and have depth perception; reach with hands and arms; pushing, pulling lifting and occasional crawling; stoop or kneel or crouch; ascend/descent ladders, stairs, scaffolding, ramps and/or poles; and lift, carry and/or move objects typically weighing up to 10-25 pounds. There may be exposure to inclement weather as well as to dirt/dust, chemicals/solvents, and noise with occasional exposure to temperature extremes and vibrations.<br/><br/><strong>TO APPLY: </strong>A resume/CV and cover letter are required. In compliance with The Americans with Disabilities Act, if selected for the interview process and accommodations are needed, please call (909) 748-8040. <strong>If you are unable to complete an application due to a disability, contact us at (909) 748-8040 to ask for an accommodation or an alternative application process.<br/><br/></strong><strong>HIRING RANGE: </strong>Anticipated hiring range is near or at the minimum of the classification, commensurate with candidate's education, experience, skills, and training. The full salary range for this position is defined as the Full Pay Range. The budgeted salary or hourly range that the University reasonably expects to pay for this position defined as the <strong>Hiring Range</strong>. Placement within the Hiring Range is determined by internal equity, and relevant qualifications.<br/><br/><strong>BACKGROUND CHECK: </strong>Satisfactory completion of a background check (including a criminal records check) is required for employment.
