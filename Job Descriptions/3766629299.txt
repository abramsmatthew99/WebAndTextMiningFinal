Please consider before submitting an application.<br/><br/>International Students: If your work authorization is through CPT, please consult your school on your ability to work 40 hours per week before applying. Our year-round program places the best students in positions where they will grow technically, professionally, and personally through their experience working closely with their Manager, Mentor, and team. We are dedicated to providing an experience that allows the intern to experience life at Tesla by including them in projects that are critical to their team’s success.<br/><br/>Qualified applicants may be reviewed by one or more of the following teams:<br/><br/>The Cell Engineering team at Tesla is looking for a highly motivated intern to contribute to fundamental improvements on our knowledge of how cells work. Tesla is a demanding and fast-paced environment where you will work with a world-class team developing Tesla’s battery technology. This role is focused on analysis and interpretation of battery tests and image data. Your work may include developing software to improve workflows, analyzing large datasets generated from our test labs, creating data visualizations, and improving the robustness of our automated analysis pipeline. The ideal candidate will combine strong knowledge of engineering fundamentals with software programming. Teamwork, analytical ability, organizational skills, and attention to detail are all highly valued skills. Individuals at every level carry a tremendous amount of responsibility and should look forward to learning an incredible amount on the job.<br/><br/>Thermal &amp; Chassis Engineering is the team behind the Tesla Heat Pump, Supermanifold, Octovalve, Superbottle, Airwave, Bioweapon Defense Mode, and more. Our multi-disciplinary team of mechanical, manufacturing, electrical, aerodynamics, thermal, and analytics engineers work together across all phases of development from idea inception, through prototyping, production, and fleet support.<br/><br/>You'll work alongside experts with many years of experience, high aptitude recent graduates and people from every stage in between, Collaboration, learning, and growth are core to the team's success.<br/><br/>We're looking for a key player who can help drive data analytics and work cross-functionally to uncover opportunities in product improvements. The candidate should have experience working with large datasets and using data to help design critical metrics, characterize engineering systems, optimize product performance, and build useful data pipelines, visualizations, and dashboards.<br/><br/><strong>What You'll Do<br/><br/></strong><li>Designing tests and analyzing data to explore fundamental battery degradation modes or drive actionable recommendations to improve vehicle product performance/quality </li><li>Identify, analyze, and interpret trends of patterns in complex data sets and depict the story via dashboards and reports </li><li>Write efficient SQL/Python code and complex queries across extensive data sets </li><li>Work effectively with engineers and conduct end-to-end analyses, from data requirement gathering, to data processing and modeling </li><li>Automate analyses and author pipelines using SQL, Python, and Airflow based ETL framework <br/><br/><br/></li><strong>What You'll Bring<br/><br/></strong><li>Pursuing Bachelor’s Degree or advanced degree in Chemical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Materials Science, Electrical Engineering, Computer Science, Statistics, or continuous integration / continuous deployment </li><li>Some familiarity with dev-ops tools: Git, Jenkins, Docker, Kubernetes, Splunk, Redis </li><li>Experience with distributed computing (Spark/Hive/Hadoop) </li><li>Experience creating data products and dashboards in Tableau, R Shiny, or D3 </li><li>First principles understanding of engineering concepts with relevant project/research experience </li><li>Excellent communication skills, both verbal and written </li><li>Ability to work independently on multiple projects with complex requirements
