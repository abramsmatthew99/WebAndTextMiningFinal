<strong>Job Description<br/><br/></strong>Job Duties: Vice President with Goldman Sachs &amp; Co. LLC in Dallas, Texas. Identify, monitor, analyze, and challenge operational risks targeting data management, governance, and technology. Provide an oversight on risks and challenges to information systems controls. Establish and maintain qualitative and quantitative operational risk appetite and tolerance with associated metrics. Maintain ongoing monitor, triggers, escalation, and mitigating actions of relevant metrics. Conduct scenario analysis by working with stakeholders to develop and quantify tail scenarios used for capital and stress testing activities. Facilitate operational risk event and data collection including reporting when necessary; and perform detailed reviews of trends to identify significant risks and ensure monitoring and remediation. Conduct quarterly trigger assessments for the division to ensure the divisions risk and control self -assessment and tail scenarios remain applicable and current. Identify organizations that have heightened data usage patterns and ensure they are covered within the Risk and Control Self Assessment (RCSA) process and are effectively challenged. Contribute to the advancement of operational risk methods and practices and the operational risk management framework inclusive of the Operational Risk framework. Identify and drive initiatives that improve the risk management activities at the firm.<br/><br/>Job Requirements: Master’s degree (U.S. or foreign equivalent) in Business Administration, Business Analytics, Computer Science, Electronic Engineering, or a related field and three (3) years of experience in the job offered or in a related role OR Bachelor’s degree (U.S. or foreign equivalent) in Business Administration, Business Analytics, Computer Science, Electronic Engineering, or a related field and five (5) years of experience in the job offered or in a related role. Prior experience must include three (3) years (with a Master’s degree) OR five (5) years (with a Bachelor’s degree) with: Working with operational risk management principles and frameworks; Working with National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) frameworks; Working with Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council (FFIEC) frameworks; Working with risk and control self-assessments (RCSAs); Working with technology processes including resiliency, change, and data management.<br/><br/>©The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc., 2023.
