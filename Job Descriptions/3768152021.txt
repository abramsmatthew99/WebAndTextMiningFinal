<strong>Position Title</strong>: Research Scientist<br/><br/><strong> Department </strong>: 2750|Sch of Computing &amp; Informatics<br/><br/><strong>Responsibilities<br/><br/></strong>The Research Scientist will work under the guidance of the Associate Professor in the School of Computing and Informatics at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette. The primary focus of this role is to conduct research aimed at advancing the state-of-the-art in cyber-physical system security within the context of Additive Manufacturing/Robotics. The role involves utilizing machine learning methods to enhance the security of 3D printer systems. Additionally, the Research Scientist will engage in interdisciplinary work involving material engineering, machine learning, and cybersecurity, contributing to mathematical modeling, coding, user studies, proposal writing, and disseminating research outcomes through publication.<br/><br/><strong>Responsibilities<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Collaborate with the Associate Professor and other team members to conduct research and investigations in the area of cyber-physical system security in Additive Manufacturing/Robotics.</li><li>Apply machine learning methods to improve the security of 3D printer systems and explore novel approaches to protect them from potential cyber threats.</li><li>Engage in interdisciplinary projects related to material/mechanical engineering, machine learning, and cybersecurity to foster innovation and advance knowledge in the field.</li><li>Develop mathematical models and algorithms to enhance the security and integrity of cyber-physical systems.</li><li>Design, implement, and test software prototypes or solutions to address security challenges in Additive Manufacturing systems/medical systems.</li><li>Analyze and interpret research data collected during experiments and studies to draw meaningful conclusions and insights.</li><li>Contribute to developing research proposals for funding agencies, including NSF, and other relevant organizations.</li><li>Collaborate with scientists, engineers, and researchers within the university and from external institutions, such as the University of Pennsylvania and the University of Florida.</li><li>Present research findings to stakeholders, including academic peers, industry professionals, and other relevant audiences.</li><li>Write research papers and reports detailing the research findings and submit them to reputable conferences and journals.</li><li>Keep up-to-date with the latest advancements and research trends in cyber-physical system security and related areas.</li><li>Undertake any additional projects and duties assigned by the Associate Professor.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Qualifications<br/><br/></strong><strong> Required Qualifications: <br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Ph.D. in Computer Science, Electrical Engineering,or a closely related field<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Preferred Qualifications<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Prior experience in conducting research related to cyber-physical system security, additive manufacturing, machine learning, or related fields.</li><li>The candidate should possess strong analytical, problem-solving, and critical-thinking skills. Proficiency in programming languages (e.g., Python, C++, etc.), mathematical modeling, data analysis, and machine learning methods is essential.</li><li>Excellent written and verbal communication skills are necessary to present research findings, collaborate with team members, and write research papers and reports.</li><li>The ability to work on interdisciplinary projects involving material engineering, machine learning, and cybersecurity is desirable.</li><li>The candidate should be able to adapt to new challenges and stay updated with the latest advancements and research trends in cyber-physical system security.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Job Ad# </strong>(req2447)
