<p><strong>Job Summary:</strong></p><p>As an Application Analyst, Epic Systems, you will serve as the primary support contact for specific Epic applications, playing a crucial role in the organization's operational health and project success. This position involves a mix of operational experience, project management, and organizational skills, ensuring that project teams remain focused and that the Epic system aligns with the organization's business needs.</p><p><br/></p><p><strong>Job Qualifications:</strong></p><p><strong>EXPERIENCE:</strong></p><p>· Clinical operations experience</p><p>· Epic end-user or application support experience</p><p>· Experience supporting clinical applications or healthcare environments.</p><p>· Epic certification preferred.</p><p><br/></p><p><strong>Job Responsibilities:</strong></p><ul><li>Act as the main support contact for the application's end-users, addressing and resolving any issues that arise.</li><li>Collaborate with various teams to identify and resolve issues impacting application performance and user experience.</li><li>Guide workflow design, system build, and testing, and tackle technical challenges associated with Epic software.</li><li>Manage system changes as per user requests and organizational needs.</li><li>Function as a liaison between end-users, Epic implementation staff, and business stakeholders to ensure system functionality meets business requirements.</li><li>Maintain regular communication with Epic representatives and participate in weekly project team meetings.</li><li>Engage with the business community and end-users to understand operational needs and direct workflow configurations.</li><li>Lead training initiatives and support end-users with troubleshooting and problem-solving.</li><li>Consistently review project status and issues with leadership, ensuring project deliverables and timelines are met.</li><li>Conduct weekly team meetings to discuss project deliverables, shared issues, user concerns, budget, and milestones.</li></ul><p><br/></p><p><strong>WORKING CONDITIONS:</strong></p><p>· Fast-paced office environment.</p><p>· After hours, weekend, and holiday work will occasionally be required as determined by the Manger of Medical Informatics to support software/hardware patches, upgrades, down times, and potential disasters.</p><p>· Work requires regular speed, accuracy, and adeptness to operate a limited range of equipment.</p><p>· Work requires handling light and simple weight material and equipment. Visual activity requires the ability to tolerate prolonged periods of close computer screens.</p><p><br/></p><p><strong>EQUIPMENT / WORK AIDS / LICENSURE:</strong></p><p>· Epic certification is preferred and will be required. Candidates will receive comprehensive training at Epic and must complete certification and tests to gain in-depth knowledge of the software.</p><p>· Some Epic application analyst roles may require a RN, Pharm. D., Licensed Medical Technologist, or other clinical certification depending on the areas supported.</p>
