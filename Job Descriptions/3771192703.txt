        Reddit is a community of communities where people can dive into anything through experiences built around their interests, hobbies, and passions. Our mission is to bring community, belonging, and empowerment to everyone in the world. Reddit users submit, vote, and comment on content, stories, and discussions about the topics they care about the most. From pets to parenting, with over 100,000 active communities and over 70 million daily active users, it is home to the most open and authentic conversations on the internet. For more information, visit redditinc.com.<br/><br/><strong>Team Description<br/><br/></strong>As a member of the user experience team, you’ll drive the understanding of user behavior, motivations and experiences with Reddit’s internal tooling. You'll partner with our product and design teams to understand internal users across regions and how we can improve their experiences. As a usability researcher, you’ll be responsible for leading usability-focused research studies across the safety and moderator teams that drive changes in user experience.<br/><br/><strong>Role Description<br/><br/></strong>You’ll investigate user behavior and user needs using qualitative research methods including interviews, journey mapping, concept testing. The ideal candidate will be able to combine their skills in research design and qualitative interviewing and analysis to improve the user experience. This is a five-month contract starting in January.<br/><br/><strong>Responsibilities<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Design and execute on unmoderated and moderated usability testing.</li><li>Lead communication and collaboration with cross-functional partners, including consumer insights, data science, design, product/program management, etc.</li><li>Develop research deliverables that achieve business and product impact.<br/><br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Basic Qualifications<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>2+ years of professional experience conducting user research</li><li>BA/BS in Psychology, Sociology, Statistics, Behavioral Science, Computer Science, Human-Computer Interaction, related fields, or equivalent experience within a product-centric environment<br/><br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Preferred Qualifications<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Previous experience conducting remote research </li><li>Understanding of strengths and shortcomings of different qualitative research methods, including when and how to apply them during each product phase.</li><li>Detail-oriented and ability to work fairly independently on a variety of tasks.</li><li>Strong interpersonal, organizational, written, and oral communication skills.</li><li>Ability to work quickly to turn around high-quality research insights; can prioritize time between multiple projects, and be flexible, adapting to changing schedules and different projects.</li><li>Experience working in ambiguity and complex cross-functional collaborations.<br/><br/><br/></li></ul>Please provide a portfolio as part of your application. Applications without portfolios will not be considered.<br/><br/><em>Reddit is committed to providing reasonable accommodations for qualified individuals with disabilities and disabled veterans in our job application procedures.
