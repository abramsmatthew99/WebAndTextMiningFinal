<strong>What To Expect<br/></strong>The Field Quality Engineering team’s mission is to improve customer experience of Tesla products by ensuring accurate data and reducing field issues, while collaborating with large cross-functional engineering teams. The primary objective of this role is to define problem statements from field failures by analyzing data and define key items to improve data accuracy.<br/><br/>The successful candidate for this position must be data driven at his/her core, have a strong quality &amp; customer-focused mindset, and have the personal drive and stamina for relentless, continual improvement (personally and professionally).<br/><br/>This role has critical objectives:<br/><li>Analyze field data to identify problem definition.</li><li>Review field data and work cross-functionally to improve data accuracy.</li><li>Work cross-functionally to help drive corrective actions and tracking of countermeasures.<br/><br/><br/></li><strong>What You'll Do<br/><br/></strong><li>Analyze service repair notes, tags, and images for top customer concerns across all vehicle models (you’ll be reading hundreds of write-ups every week)</li><li>Define problem statements for field concerns</li><li>Review accuracy of service write-ups and dashboard systems</li><li>Provide weekly summaries for top service failures to drive factory/design improvements</li><li>Work cross-functionally with teams to improve data accuracy of field concerns</li><li>Monitor product field performance and provide trend analysis to support strategic decisions</li><li>Participate in continuous improvement activities and take preventive actions.<br/><br/><br/></li><strong>What You'll Bring<br/><br/></strong><li>3 - 5 years’ experience in a manufacturing or automotive environment, customer-facing role, or data analyst role; or bachelor’s degree in a technical field, computer science, business analytics, or related</li><li>Prior applicable experience desired; preferably in a hands-on role such as Manufacturing / Quality / Service</li><li>Exceptional problem solving and Failure Analysis, strong attention to detail and analytical thinking.</li><li>Experience with Excel and PowerPoint required, or proven track record of quickly learning new digital programs</li><li>Strong written communication skills, able to tailor communication between non-technical and technical audiences</li><li>A passion and curiosity for data, systems and data driven decision making</li><li>Ability to use tools and business system applications to manage, synthesize, and interpret data.</li><li>Programming Languages &amp; Tools: SQL, Python, Minitab, JMP or similar are a strong plus</li><li>Must be detail oriented, effectively handle multiple priorities, have a strong quality mindset and have the ability to meet tight deadlines.<br/><br/><br/></li><strong>Benefits<br/></strong><strong>Compensation and Benefits<br/></strong>Along with competitive pay, as a full-time Tesla employee, you are eligible for the following benefits at day 1 of hire:<br/><br/><ul><ul><li> Aetna PPO and HSA plans &gt; 2 medical plan options with $0 payroll deduction</li><li> Family-building, fertility, adoption and surrogacy benefits</li><li> Dental (including orthodontic coverage) and vision plans, both have options with a $0 paycheck contribution</li><li> Company Paid (Health Savings Account) HSA Contribution when enrolled in the High Deductible Aetna medical plan with HSA</li><li> Healthcare and Dependent Care Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA)</li><li> LGBTQ+ care concierge services</li><li> 401(k) with employer match, Employee Stock Purchase Plans, and other financial benefits</li><li> Company paid Basic Life, AD&amp;D, short-term and long-term disability insurance</li><li> Employee Assistance Program</li><li> Sick and Vacation time (Flex time for salary positions), and Paid Holidays</li><li> Back-up childcare and parenting support resources</li><li> Voluntary benefits to include: critical illness, hospital indemnity, accident insurance, theft &amp;
