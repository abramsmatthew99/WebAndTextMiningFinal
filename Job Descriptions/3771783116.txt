        Job Responsibilities<br/><br/><strong>Who We Are<br/><br/></strong>The South Carolina State Library (SCSL) serves the people of South Carolina by supporting state government and libraries to provide opportunities for learning in a changing environment. We develop, support, and sustain a thriving statewide community of learners committed to making South Carolina stronger.<br/><br/><strong>Job Opening<br/><br/></strong>We have a great career opportunity available for a <strong>Federal Documents Librarian </strong>in our Columbia, South Carolina headquarters.<br/><br/>Under general supervision of the Director of Digitization and Government Documents, performs all necessary duties of a selective Federal depository library related to the acquisition, processing, cataloging, maintenance, and promotion of Federal documents received from the Government Publishing Office (GPO) through the Federal Depository Library Program (FDLP).<br/><br/>The candidate must have excellent customer service skills with the ability to interact courteously and effectively with internal and external customers. They should have excellent organizational skills, attention to detail and the ability to handle multiple projects and meet deadlines under limited supervision. They must have the ability to communicate effectively, both orally and in writing with all levels of staff and management.<br/><br/><strong>Major Responsibilities<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Receives and processes shipments for all newly arriving Federal print documents from the FDLP. </li><li>Accurately performs cataloging of new and historical print Federal documents in local catalog using OCLC; adds holdings records in catalog for Federal documents using the SuDoc classification system; uploads into the local catalog monthly MARC file of new electronic Federal documents received from Marcive through the Cataloging Record Distribution Program (CRDP).</li><li>Conducts all tasks required by role as FDLP Coordinator. Reviews, evaluates and maintains depository selection profile of Federal documents received from GPO. Maintains the U.S. Government Information resource guide on State Library website. </li><li>Completes requested depository surveys (Bi-Annual and CRDP); communicates with GPO regarding library status, as needed; follows all best practices and regulations required for Federal documents received through the FDLP. </li><li>Performs regular collection maintenance of physical Federal documents collection: maintains records in local catalog, edits records, shelves, shifts, reorganizes, and re-houses items as needed. </li><li>Monitors Association of Southeastern Research Libraries (ASERL) and FDLP offers lists for Federal publications about South Carolina or those of local interest. Identifies documents in the Federal collection to post on ASERL offers list.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>If You Are Looking For An Organization That Offers<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>a collaborative and inclusive work environment</li><li> an excellent work culture </li><li>paid professional memberships</li><li>professional development and learning opportunities for staff</li><li>tuition assistance</li><li>an opportunity to work with professionals that are passionate about delivering exceptional results and advancing the mission and vision of their organization<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Then South Carolina State Library is the organization for you!<br/><br/></strong><strong>What We Believe<br/><br/></strong>We outwardly demonstrate who we are as an organization through our values. Our people are our greatest resource and our values drive our people.<br/><br/><ul><li> Communication: We believe communication in every direction and through multiple methods is essential to doing our work at the highest level. As an information organization, we use a transparent process for decision making which encourages creativity and fosters collaboration among staff and our community. </li><li> Flexibility: An agile organization is one that can change course to provide better services. We define our organization in this way and pride ourselves in being good stewards of all resources. </li><li> Community Driven: Our community is the state. Our value is demonstrated in what we are able to give back as public servants. We believe that by remaining open to our community’s needs, we can personalize experiences for individuals and groups. </li><li> Inclusivity: We strive to expand opportunities to better connect with others and break down barriers. Integrity, respect, and equality guide our performance and we are committed to hiring staff that reflect our community. </li><li> Professionalism: Our staff model professionalism by providing a welcoming environment, excellent customer service, valuing diversity, and supporting multiple methods of service delivery. A well-trained and knowledgeable staff is our greatest asset. <br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Need more reasons to apply? <br/><br/></strong>SCSL is committed to providing our employees with a collaborative and inclusive work environment that provides challenging work, personal and professional growth, and a work/life balance.<br/><br/>In addition to all the great perks we’ve already shared, we also offer:<br/><br/><ul><li>Health, Dental, Vision, Life, Retirement, and more</li><li>15 days of annual leave per year</li><li>15 days of sick leave per year</li><li>13 paid holidays</li><li>Team Building Activities – We believe in growing together <br/><br/></li></ul>Don’t miss this great opportunity to take your career to the next level! Apply today.<br/><br/>As an EEO/Affirmative Action Employer all qualified applicants will receive consideration for employment without regard to race, color, religion, age (over 40), sex, including pregnancy and childbirth (or related medical conditions), sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, disability, or veteran status.<br/><br/><strong>Minimum And Additional Requirements<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>A bachelor's degree in library science, information science or related field and experience in library systems and operations.</li><li>Ability to lift and move materials, bending and reaching to perform regular collection maintenance. <br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Preferred Qualifications<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>A master’s degree in library science from an ALA accredited college or university.</li><li>3 years professional library experience.</li><li>Experience working with government documents.</li><li>Knowledge of library classification systems and standard cataloging rules.</li><li>Knowledge of Marc and RDA copy cataloging. </li><li>Organizational skills and attention to detail.<br/><br/></li></ul>Additional Comments<br/><br/>Work takes place in an office environment. Overnight travel is not expected for this position, however may be occasionally necessary. Some local travel is expected. Occasional work on evenings and weekends.<br/><br/><ul><li>Supplemental questions are considered part of your official application.
