It enables the critical life-cycle of access, creation, dissemination, and preservation of knowledge.<br/><br/><strong>About The Position<br/><br/></strong>Provides management of the library's web and digital services area. Contributes to IT projects in the library and provides other services as required. The Web Developer also consults and works collaboratively with library faculty and staff, instructional faculty, researchers and students on a variety of digital projects.<br/><br/><strong>Responsibilities<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Manages libraries web presence: Ensures an effective web presence for the Libraries through content management and utilization of appropriate web platforms and UX best practices. Manages timelines and deliverables for assigned digital projects and initiatives. Serves as team member on digital project management groups as appropriate; and</li><li>Contributes to the integration of library web platforms via APIs, REST and/or other(s). Provides development assistance to library repositories (e.g., DSpace, Dataverse, etc.).<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Required Qualifications<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Bachelor's degree in computer science, or an ALA-accredited master's degree in library or information science, or an equivalent certified foreign degree with a significant concentration in information systems and related technologies;</li><li>Experience designing, developing and maintaining database-driven websites or services;</li><li>Experience with LAMP technologies (Linux, Apache, MySQL, PHP/PERL/PYTHON);</li><li>Knowledge of the role of academic libraries supporting student success, research productivity, and community engagement;</li><li>Single-user level UNIX system administration skills (e.g., shell competency);</li><li>Ability to work effectively as part of team; and</li><li>Ability to exercise independent judgement and problem solving.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Preferred Qualifications<br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Master's degree in related field;</li><li>Substantive experience with LAMP technologies (Linux, Apache, MySQL, PHP/PERL/PYTHON);</li><li>Experience working in an academic library supporting student success, research productivity, and community engagement;</li><li>General knowledge of computer programming and data structures;</li><li>Proficiency with at least one CMS (e.g., Drupal, WordPress, Omeka, Joomla);</li><li>UNIX system administration skills beyond those of a single user;</li><li>Advanced competency in at least one scripting language (Python, PERL, PHP, JavaScript); and</li><li>API development skills and facility with appropriate tools/methods (e.g., JSON, REST, XML).<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Instructions To Applicants<br/><br/></strong>For full consideration, applicants must apply for <strong>Web Developer</strong> at https://jobs.gmu.edu/;
