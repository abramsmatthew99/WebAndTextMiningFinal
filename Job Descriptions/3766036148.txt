        Job Description:<br/><br/><p><strong>Job Title</strong> Technology Infrastructure Principal Auditor</p><p><strong>Corporate Title </strong>Assistant Vice President</p><p><strong>Location </strong>Cary, NC</p><p> </p><p><strong>Who we are:</strong></p><p>In short - an essential part of Deutsche Bank's technology solution, developing applications for key business areas.</p><p> </p><p>Our Technologists drive Cloud, Cyber and business technology strategy while transforming it within a robust, hands-on engineering culture. Learning is a key element of our people strategy, and we have a variety of options for you to develop professionally. Our approach to the future of work champions flexibility and is rooted in the understanding that there have been dramatic shifts in the ways we work.</p><p> </p><p>Having first established a presence in the Americas in the 19th century, Deutsche Bank opened its US technology center in Cary, North Carolina in 2009. Learn more about us here.</p><p> </p><p><strong>Overview</strong></p><p>In your role as Technology Infrastructure Principal Auditor, you will carry out the planning, preparation, co-ordination, and execution of audits, mainly in the area of information</p><p>technology (IT), security, databases, networks and support Cyber infrastructure as well as IT application auditors with technology infrastructure related topics. The Group Audit (GA) function comprises over 700 staff located in our four hub locations New York, London, Frankfurt, Singapore and 26 other countries across the globe. The function is a professional, business focused, proactive risk-based global audit team that operates with transparency, integrity, independence, and assists the Bank's business and infrastructure areas to identify key control weaknesses.</p><p> </p><p><strong>What We Offer You </strong></p><ul><li><p>A diverse and inclusive environment that embraces change, innovation, and collaboration</p></li><li><p>A hybrid working model, allowing for in-office / work from home flexibility, generous vacation, personal and volunteer days</p></li><li><p>Employee Resource Groups support an inclusive workplace for everyone and promote community engagement</p></li><li><p>Competitive compensation packages including health and wellbeing benefits, retirement savings plans, parental leave, and family building benefits</p></li><li><p>Educational resources, matching gift, and volunteer programs</p></li></ul><p> </p><p><strong>What You'll Do </strong></p><ul><li><p>Evaluate the adequacy and effectiveness of controls relating to technology risks and communicating findings to the responsible management/stakeholder teams</p></li><li><p>Planning, preparing, coordinating, and executing audits to evaluate the adequacy and effectiveness of controls within the Information Technology and security</p></li><li><p>Support the implementation of the GA risk-based methodology including; performing coverage of Infrastructure technology processes and the respective cyber technology environment along with supporting structured ongoing risk assessments via the Continuous Audit Assessment process</p></li><li><p>Undertaking audit assignments, drafting, and consolidating audit reports for review by Audit Management and facilitate issue tracking and validating the closure of audit findings</p></li><li><p>Serve as a subject matter expert for colleagues on issues or questions related to within GA</p></li><li><p>Work as a teammate on a global team, interacting with colleagues in Singapore, Frankfurt, London, and the United States</p></li></ul><p> </p><p><strong>Skills You'll Need </strong></p><ul><li><p>Bachelor's degree level with a concentration in Computer Science (or equivalent qualification / work experience required), Master's degree preferred</p></li><li><p>Have certifications demonstrating familiarity with Technology Information, Security and audit practices; e.g. CISSP, CISA, CISM, CIPP/IT, SANS GIAC, CCNA</p></li><li><p>Relevant experience of audit experience in the following topics: Information Security, IT infrastructure, IT production, IT operations, and IT Vendor Risk Assessments</p></li><li><p>Relevant experience of analyzing and articulating IT risks from current threat vectors, combined with technical and process skills in an enterprise environment</p></li><li><p>Be familiar with various Information Technology Frameworks; e.g. FFIEC handbook, CAT tool, CoBit, ISO 27k, NIST 800 series, CSC Top 20 Security Controls and be able to use these as guides to evaluate the state of controls within the Bank. Have experience in performing audits and security assessments, with particular focus on technology infrastructure, cyber security, information security, cloud technology, technology management, governance, and compliance with regulatory and policy requirements</p></li></ul><p> </p><p><strong>Skills That Will Help You Excel</strong></p><ul><li><p>Must have a good understanding of IT controls, security control processes, information security and regulatory requirements in the financial sector</p></li><li><p>Pro-actively developing and maintaining professional working relationships with colleagues, the Business and respective support areas</p></li><li><p>You will be expected to remain constantly engaged with emerging and technology trends and remain abreast of the general cyber environment</p></li><li><p>Be adaptable, pro-active, self-sufficient and innovative with strong organizational skills to take ownership and responsibility of agreed targets, meeting time-lines within budget to enable a timely and efficient completion of audit projects</p></li><li><p>Show ability to multi-task assignments, prioritize workload with limited supervision and be able to perform under pressure delivering to deadlines without sacrificing on quality</p></li></ul><p> </p><p><strong>Expectations</strong></p><p>It is the Bank's expectation that employees hired into this role will work in the Cary, NC office in accordance with the Bank's hybrid working model.</p><p>The salary range for this position in Cary is $80,000 to $120,000<em>. </em>Actual salaries may be based on a number of factors including, but not limited to, a candidate's skill set, experience, education, work location and other qualifications.
