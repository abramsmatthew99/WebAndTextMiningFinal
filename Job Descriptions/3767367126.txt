<strong>Summary<br/><br/></strong>The employee will be responsible for the Information Technology (IT) systems in the hospital. The employee will carry-out her/his duties by adhering to the highest standards of ethical and moral conduct, acts in the best interest of Loretto Hospital, and fully supports the mission, vision, and values of Loretto Hospital. The employee will have a solid IT background and knowledge of health care IT systems, and have demonstrated ability to interact, relate to, work with, and support the activities of a diverse workforce.<br/><br/><strong>ESSENTIAL DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES include the following. Other duties may be assigned<br/><br/></strong><ul><li> Set objectives and strategies for the IT department</li><li> Provide leadership, direction, coordination, and administration to the IT staff</li><li> Select and implement suitable technology to streamline all internal operations and help optimize their strategic benefits</li><li> Design and customize technological systems and platforms to improve customer experience</li><li> Plan the implementation of new systems and provide guidance to IT professionals and other staff within the organization</li><li> Approve purchases of technological equipment and software and establish partnerships with IT providers</li><li> Oversee the technological infrastructure (networks and computer systems) in the organization to ensure optimal performance</li><li> Direct and organize IT-related projects</li><li> Monitor changes or advancements in technology to discover ways the company can gain competitive advantage</li><li> Analyze the costs, value and risks of information technology to advise management and suggest actions<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Compliance Responsibilities<br/><br/></strong><ul><li> Understands and adheres to Loretto Hospital’s compliance standards as they appear in the Compliance Policy, Code of Conduct and Conflict of Interest Policy.</li><li> Keeps abreast of all pertinent federal, state and Hospital regulations, laws, and policies as they presently exist and as they change or are modified.</li><li> Ensures that the staff are trained and evaluated on their knowledge of and adherence to compliance policies and procedures specific to their jobs.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Job Requirements<br/><br/></strong>To perform this job successfully, an individual must be able to perform each essential duty satisfactorily. The requirements listed must be representative of the knowledge, skills, minimum education, training, licensure, experience, and/or ability required. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform the essential functions.<br/><br/><ul><li> Master’s degree in Computer Science</li><li> Certification preferred: PMP, MCSE, A+, CCNA, MCP, Network+, CISSP, MCSA, ITIL or Security+ preferred</li><li> 7 years' experience in software development</li><li> 5 years' experience in senior leadership in a healthcare environment</li><li> Experience in Meditech highly desirable<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Physical Demands<br/><br/></strong>Able to lift 50 to 75 pounds, climb stairs, climb ladders, tolerate temperature shifts between environments, sit for extend periods of time.<br/><br/><strong>The above statements are intended to describe the general nature and level of work being performed by people assigned to this classification. They are not intended to be construed as an exhaustive list of all responsibilities, duties and skills required of the personnel so classified.<br/><br/></strong><strong>For purposes of the American with Disabilities Act, “Job Duties” are essential job functions.</strong>
