<strong>Job Description<br/><br/></strong>This is what you'll do:<br/><br/><ul><li>Design and develop custom electronic test equipment to support end of line and validation testing of in-house built onboard chargers, DC-DC converters, inverters, and other power electronics equipment. </li><li>Design and develop functional test equipment for printed circuit board assemblies (PCBA). </li><li>Document test equipment designs via electrical wiring schematics such as AutoCAD electrical</li><li>Work closely with the engineering project teams to ensure test equipment designs align with the software and hardware requirements</li><li>Participate in design reviews, requirement reviews, DFMEAs, PFMEAs and Hazard Analyses</li><li>Develop test procedures defining specific test setup requirements and instructions</li><li>Perform data analysis and generate reports. </li><li>Support technical root cause analysis and resolution of issues found during testing</li><li>Utilize standard electronic test equipment such as power supplies, oscilloscopes, multimeters, signal generators and power analyzers</li><li>Use sensors such as thermocouples, RTDs, strain gauges, accelerometers, pressure gauges, and flow meters<br/><br/></li></ul>This is what you'll need:<br/><br/><ul><li>BS. in Electrical/Electronic Engineering, Computer Science, or a related field. </li><li>2+ years' experience designing or troubleshooting test equipment for high voltage power electronics or chargers. </li><li>Ability to create schematics using design software tools such as AutoCAD Electrical. </li><li>Familiarity with modular test instruments and National Instruments I/O Systems (PXI, cDAQ)</li><li>Knowledge of computer hardware, communication, and bus protocols (RS232, CAN, GPIB, Ethernet, USB). </li><li>Hands-on experience with diagnosing and troubleshooting complex electrical issues</li><li>Experience in scripting languages such as shell and bash highly prefered. </li><li>Ability to work in a fast-paced development environment. </li><li>Self-motivated: Demonstrated ability to set direction and guide the team towards it. </li><li>PCB design experience preferred</li></ul>
</div>
