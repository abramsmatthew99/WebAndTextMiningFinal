Minimum QualificationsNon-competitive: one year of permanent service as a Data Analyst 2 OR bachelor’sdegree in data or computer science, informatics, economics, statistics, mathematics, ora related field, and five years of experience* in data visualization, and statistical models and data mining tools.Preferred Qualifications: Experience using data-oriented programming languages such as SAS, Python, R, and statistical computations, and visualization software to support data management, quality control and improvement for several large data systems. Experience with data analytics and engineering technologies. Familiarity with healthcare and public health data and standards such HL7, X12, ICD and experience with leadership and supervision.Excellent oral and written communication skills, including the ability to clearly articulate data analytics, engineering, and management concepts to a wide variety of audience.Duties DescriptionThe Data Analyst 3 position will drive the design, building, and implementation of new data models and data pipelines from ingestion to consumption for several large data systems overseen by the Center for Health Data Innovation including the State Health Information Network for NY (SHIN-NY); the All-Payer Database (APD); the Statewide Planning and Research Cooperative System (SPARCS); the Provider Network Data System (PNDS); and the Health Data New York (HealthDataNY) platform.The Data Analyst 3 will oversee the Data Engineering Unit under the direction of the Director of the Bureau of Informatics and Technologies in Center for Health Data Innovation. The Data Engineering Unit is responsible for developing and operating large scale, enterprise data solutions and application that helps to guide policy and decision-making in healthcare and public health.The incumbent will provide informatics leadership in the areas of planning, development, preparation and implementation of programs targeting specific disease conditions or implementing new care practices aimed at improving population and public health by improving health care quality and health outcomes.The Data Analyst 3 will lead staff including Data Analyst 2 and 1 in performing detail assessments of current data systems and technologies and creating an appropriate transition path to further the department’s use of these data and systems to answer questions during public health crises, contribute to research, and support policy analysis.The incumbent will build cross-functional relationships with both internal and external stakeholders to understand data needs and deliver innovative and effective dashboards and data visualizations to promote health care quality and patient safety.Duties include, but are not limited to: Oversee data engineering activities for large data systems overseen by the Center for Health Data Innovation; Define the processes needed to achieve data engineering excellence in all areas, including project management and system reliability; Manage data engineering plans across major data systems in the Center for Health Data Innovation; Ensure performance management, code optimization for data engineering activities and partner with other state agencies, DOH programs and vendors to continuously support data infrastructure for the Center for Health Data Innovation; Direct data engineering support for major quality, patient safety and public health initiatives; Assess the state of current data systems and technologies in the Center for Health Data Innovation and modernize them as needed to support the analytic needs of the department and other stakeholders; Design, develop, and manage cloud based data analytics technology solutions; Collaborate with and across DOH programs to design, develop, test, implement, and support data analytics solutions in full-stack development tools and technologies; Provide actionable recommendations for agency executive staff and center leadership regarding data analytics investments and implementation strategies; and Perform full range of supervisory responsibilities and other duties as assigned.Additional CommentsBenefits of Working for NYSGenerous benefits package, worth 65% of salary, including:Holiday & Paid Time Off Thirteen (13) paid holidays annually Up to Thirteen (13) days of paid vacation leave annually Up to Five (5) days of paid personal leave annually Up to Thirteen (13) days of paid sick leave annually for PEF/CSEA Up to Eight (8) days of paid sick leave annually for M/C Up to three (3) days of professional leave annually to participate in professional developmentHealth Care Benefits Eligible employees and dependents can pick from a variety of affordable health insurance programs Family dental and vision benefits at no additional costAdditional Benefits New York State Employees’ Retirement System (ERS) Membership NYS Deferred Compensation Access to NY 529 and NY ABLE College Savings Programs, as well as U.S. Savings Bonds Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) And many more..For more information on the NYS Department of Health’s Mission, Vision, Values and Strategic Plan, please visit: https://health.ny.gov/commissioner/index.htm
