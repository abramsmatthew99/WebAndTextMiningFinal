<strong>Introduction <br/><br/></strong>The Wisconsin Department of Veterans Affairs (WDVA) currently has an opening for an IS Technical Services Senior (Desktop/Help Desk Support Specialist) position. This position is headquartered out of our Central Office location in Madison on Rimrock Road, conveniently located 5 minutes from downtown Madison, just off the Beltline and the Capitol City bike path.<br/><br/>WDVA employees have access to an excellent comprehensive benefit and compensation package that includes:<br/><br/><ul><li> Substantial leave time including at least 3.5 weeks of vacation to start, 9 paid holidays, and ample accrued sick time that rolls over each year </li><li> Excellent and affordable health, vision, and dental benefits </li><li> An exceptional pension plan with employer match and lifetime retirement payment, plus an optional tax advantaged 457 retirement savings plan </li><li> A flexible hybrid remote work schedule (up to 80%) with the option to alternate time in the office with remote work. Remote work opportunities will be discussed in greater detail during the interview process. <br/><br/></li></ul>WDVA employees may also be eligible for Public Service Loan Forgiveness . To learn more about WDVA’s complete compensation package, please visit our Total Rewards Calculator !<br/><br/>The State of Wisconsin is an Equal Opportunity and Affirmative Action employer seeking a diverse and talented workforce.<br/><br/><strong>Position Summary<br/><br/></strong>This position is responsible for the independent resolution of technical issues; working with vendors; identifying past problems, resolutions, and trends. Technical support at this level includes hardware and software support, phone system and mobile device support, mobile device management, support of Microsoft 365 and other desktop software and Help Desk support.<br/><br/>To view the complete position description, click here .<br/><br/><strong> Salary Information <br/><br/></strong>The starting salary for this position will be between $68,640 and $74,880 annually ($33.00 and $36.00 an hour), depending on candidate qualifications, plus an attractive employee benefits package.<br/><br/>This classification is in pay schedule and range 07-34. This level is exempt from the overtime provisions of the federal Fair Labor Standards Act and will be paid on a salary basis.<br/><br/>For current State employees, compensation will be set in accordance with the State Compensation Plan.<br/><br/>A 12-month probation period will be required.<br/><br/><strong> Job Details <br/><br/></strong>WDVA’s computer systems must be operational 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. This may require work schedule changes to resolve problems affecting normal operations. A non-standard work schedule may be occasionally required to resolve computer problems during unscheduled work hours to ensure WDVA computer systems remain operational.<br/><br/>Applicants must be legally authorized to work in the United States (i.e., a citizen or national of the U.S., a lawful permanent resident, an alien authorized to work in the U.S. without WDVA sponsorship) at the time of application.<br/><br/>Due to the nature of this position, WDVA will conduct criminal background checks prior to an offer of hire.<br/><br/><strong>Qualifications<br/><br/></strong>In their resume and/or letter, <strong> <strong> minimally qualified applicants </strong> </strong> will provide details on their experience with <strong> <strong>:</strong> <br/><br/></strong><ul><li> Providing computer hardware and software technical support (e.g. troubleshoot and resolve issues, answer technical questions, install and configure hardware and software, etc.); and </li><li> Performing help desk functions (e.g. receive and log support calls, refer requests to proper staff, identify support trends, etc.); and <br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Well-qualified applicants </strong> will also provide details on their experience with:<br/><br/><ul><li> Training users on computer-related topics; and/or </li><li> Mobile device management; and/or </li><li> An Associates Degree or higher in Computer Science or a similar field of study <br/><br/></li></ul>Additional required experience that will be assessed later in the selection process are:<br/><br/><ul><li> Providing customer service <br/><br/></li></ul><strong>How To Apply<br/><br/></strong>To be considered for this position, you will need to complete the online application process. For instructions, refer to the following link: Applying to State Service . You will be required to attach a current resume and letter of qualifications.<br/><br/><strong>Your letter of qualifications and resume are very important parts of your application and are used during our evaluation process to determine your qualifications as they relate to the job. </strong>For instructions on developing your resume and letter of qualifications for your application and what should be included in these materials, click here .<br/><br/>Submitted materials will be evaluated and the most qualified applicants will be invited to participate in the next step of the hiring process for available vacancies.<br/><br/>Permanent classified state employees who are eligible for consideration (transfer, voluntary demotion, or promotion) should complete the application process as described above by the stated deadline.<br/><br/>Veterans with a 30% or greater service-related disability rating are eligible for non-competitive appointment. If you meet this eligibility criteria, please send a letter of qualifications, resume, DD-214 (honorable discharge), and documentation of your service-connected disability rating (dated within 12 months) to the contact listed below. Current state employees in permanent positions are not eligible for non-competitive appointment.<br/><br/>Questions can be directed to Evan Holzhueter at evan.holzhueter@wisconsin.gov .<br/><br/><strong> Deadline to Apply <br/><br/></strong>All application materials must be received by <strong>11:59 PM on</strong> <strong>December 22, 2023</strong>.
