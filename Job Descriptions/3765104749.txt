        We are seeking a talented and motivated Data Scientist II to join our dynamic team. In this remote position, you will play a crucial role in leveraging advanced data analytics, big data, and cutting-edge data science technology to drive innovation and enhance healthcare outcomes. As a Data Scientist II, you will be responsible for applying data science techniques to both structured and unstructured data sets, developing algorithms, and contributing to the overall success of our healthcare initiatives.<br/><br/><strong>Key Responsibilities<br/><br/></strong>Predictive Modeling: Design and develop data models to predict member outcomes and anticipate the future impact of key member decisions.<br/><br/>Analysis Tools: Create and implement analysis tools that extract, analyze, and store data in formats tailored to meet specific business needs.<br/><br/>Exploratory Data Analysis: Conduct exploratory data analysis from complex data sources, building key data sets that align with the Company's mission.<br/><br/>Metrics Monitoring: Design experiments to monitor key metrics, identifying opportunities for improvement.<br/><br/>Model Development: Develop mathematical and statistical models to discern relevant content, events, and patterns.<br/><br/>Communication and Presentation: Actively participate in presentations, effectively communicating analysis results and key findings.<br/><br/>Operational Analytics: Contribute to the design of automated operational analytics processes to achieve scalability and durability in analysis processes.<br/><br/>Health Management Programs: Validate and measure the outcomes of health management programs using tools such as SAS, R, and others, working with provider data, claims data, and membership data.<br/><br/>Project Management: Manage multiple projects concurrently, ensuring timely and successful completion.<br/><br/>Training Support: Assist in training Data Analysts, sharing your expertise to foster skill development within the team.<br/><br/><strong>Qualifications<br/><br/></strong>Education: Master's degree in Statistics, Mathematics, Computer Science, Informatics, Econometrics, Engineering, or Experimental Science with 3+ years of experience. Alternatively, a Bachelor's degree and 5+ years of quantitative analysis experience in data science capabilities.<br/><br/>Technical Skills: Proficient in data mining, predictive modeling, machine learning, statistical modeling, and large-scale data acquisition. Experience with database technologies, including Oracle, SAP, DB2, Teradata, MS SQL Server, SAP HANA, and MySQL.<br/><br/>Location:<br/><br/>This is a remote position, and candidates must reside in the United States.<br/><br/>If you are passionate about leveraging data to make a positive impact in healthcare and possess the skills and experience outlined above, we invite you to apply and join our innovative team.<br/><br/>Employment Type: Full-Time
