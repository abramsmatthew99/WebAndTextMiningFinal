We are seeking a Chief Information Officer with one of our clients in Olympia, WA Job Description:As the Chief Information Officer (CIO) for the Information Technology Services Division (ITSD), you’ll have the opportunity to put your expertise, knowledge, and skills to use as you lead a cultural, business, and functional transformation. In this role, you’ll engage staff in developing and implementing IT strategies and services that have an enterprise-wide impact. We are passionate about our customers, both internal and external, it will be critical that you cultivate a deep understanding of the businesses you support and align resources to move the needle. With complex, competing business needs, you’ll be relied upon to foster the continued development of a strong governance model, ensuring priorities are achieved.We are looking for an enthusiastic leader who builds trust and leads with integrity, empowering customers, and staff. As a leader, you’ll ensure the delivery of quality IT services by investing in and driving core processes, prioritization, and operational excellence. You will direct the deployment of new technology as you strive for innovation, continuous improvement, and growth in expanding our services. This includes utilizing your project management skills in managing inefficiencies, monitoring budgets and expenditures, and assessing contracts for technology products and services.In addition, the division is pursuing expanded outreach and support for diverse communities seeking access and insight into data sources that impact equity in Washington. To drive this work forward, you’ll need to demonstrate emotional intelligence, be willing to listen, and be open to differences, to ensure everyone has a seat at the table.Duties:Provide leadership, coaching, and direction to the IT leadership team and staff. Lead the development of the IT strategy and roadmap. Ensure its integration with the enterprise’s strategic planning process and oversee the information technology organization to meet business needs. Recommend and implement processes to prioritize projects, manage resources, and make IT investment decisions.Act as a trusted advisor in building and maintaining relationships with other executives and business unit leaders to develop a clear understanding of business needs. Ensure cost-effective delivery of IT services to meet those needs and respond with agility to changing business priorities.Direct the design and implementation of the information and technology operating model, organizational structure, and governance process. Use influencing and negotiation skills to create synergies across the enterprise to enable cost-effective and innovative shared solutions in achievement of business goals.Stay current on new technologies and platforms and provide direction on what emerging technologies should be assimilated, integrated, and introduced within the enterprise to ensure IT capabilities respond to the needs of the enterprise’s digital business strategy.Provide strategic direction on the IT organization’s innovation efforts and champion experimenting with new solutions.Develop and control annual revenue and operating expenditure budget for IT to ensure the budget is consistent with the enterprise’s overall strategic objectives and is within the plan.Serve on enterprise planning and policymaking committees. Drive the development of enterprise technology standards, governance processes, and performance metrics to ensure IT delivers value to the enterprise.Sets up project management/support methodologies, knowledge management, vendor management, asset procurement/management, and setting hardware and software standards.Review technology portfolio for alignment with business needs; eliminate waste and fill gaps.Coordinate with Washington Technology Solutions (WaTech) and other service providers to ensure that purchased services are effectively integrated and managed to support OFM operations in a reliable and cost-effective manner.Develop and implement IT strategies and initiatives. Present these plans to leadership teams at all levels of the agency for business alignment, support, and awareness.Engage with One Washington Program leadership toward the successful implementation of a new Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) solution to replace legacy enterprise administrative systems.Chair the Information Technology Steering Committee (ITSC); the chief governing body for all OFM IT resourcing and investment decisions.Actively engage with colleagues on the senior leadership team. Provide insights and updates to influence agency-wide decision-making around policies and practices.Communicate IT strategy in forums with agency staff. Enlists the division in efforts to test and promote agency-wide initiatives.Required Qualifications:Bachelor's degree or higher in computer science, information systems, business administration, or a closely related field. Please Note: Each additional year of qualifying management experience may substitute year for year for the education.Ten (10) years of experience in information technology or related management experience in the industry.Five (5) years of leadership responsibilities.Commitment to championing and living the values of Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, and Belonging as a leader and as a key communicator.Demonstrated experience with the following:Leadership of IT transformational initiatives in complex and dynamic environments and taking advantage of digital opportunities to innovate business models and enable the digitalization of the business.Strategy, decision-making, and management of an information technology environment including application development, production operating services, third-party software systems, information security, customer technology support, data management, and telecommunications.Plan, direct, implement, monitor, and evaluate the functional and technical components of information technology services, including the management and decision-making around the viability of legacy systems.Consulting, advising, and presenting to executive management on technology services solutions, policies, and strategic planning.Leading significant, complex change across organizations.Driving strong prioritization, business planning and divisional budget management.Implementing OCIO policies and regulatory and statutory requirements.Driving organizational change that effectively balances the needs for continuously exploring capabilities to optimize operational efficiency, with delivering innovative and agile IT solutions to enable the business.Desired Qualifications:Five (5) years of experience in related, industry-leading, large, cross-functional teams or enterprise-wide programs, influencing senior-level management and key stakeholders effectively across the organization and within complex contexts.Experience in Agile Development, with specific Program Management (or similar) experience.Understanding of product-oriented operating model, including relationship with Product Owner, Scrum Master, and rest of technical team.What we offer our employees:An environment of diversity, equity, inclusion, and belonging. We strive to hire, cultivate, and retain a competitive workforce that reflects the communities we serve.A healthy life/work balance by offering flexible/alternative work schedules, wellness programs, mobile arrangements, and telework opportunities.Employees (and their families) have coverage options for medical, dental, vision, and basic life insurance.Opportunity to enroll in state retirement programs and a deferred compensation plan.A minimum of 14 days of vacation leave per year and 12 paid holidays throughout the calendar year.A commute trip reduction program, tuition reimbursement, and the opportunity to join statewide Business Resource Groups.
