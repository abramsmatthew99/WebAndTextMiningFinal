May act as project leader having duties of instructing, directing and reviewing the work of other operating system personnel engaged in the specific project; performs complex technical functions and administrative work in installing, monitoring &amp; evaluating technical hardware and software; plans implements, coordinates, the installation of technical hardware and software functions for the Networking area and Local Area Networks.<br/><br/><strong>Duties and Responsibilities:<br/><br/></strong>Typical duties include, but are not limited to, a combination of functions defined in the lower level of this series AND such functions as:<br/><br/><ul><li>Interfaces and maintains a working relationship with all SOS user departments in Chicago and various trades personnel to coordinate large scale network modifications and enhancements; performs large scale network complex technical duties in the installation and maintenance of hardware and software configurations; which includes communications test equipment, concentrators, terminal equipment, local area network hardware, and preparation of necessary cabling to connect system components; assists lower level staff in the installation and maintenance of personal computer systems requiring such services; and in the evaluation of hardware and software implementations or enhancements as to its impact on the communications network;</li><li>Serve as an assistant to the supervisor by assigning/scheduling work, participating in performance evaluations, and advising employees on work performance; monitors for quality, compliance, and adherence to deadlines;</li><li>Conducts and coordinates training programs and retraining of central and local office staff, participates in determining training needs; and provides application support for productivity software and network operating systems;</li><li>Receives inquires dispatched by the Help Desk regarding system problems, determines nature and extent of problems and takes the necessary corrective action dealing with identifying and resolving terminal, circuit and network malfunctions, both hardware and software;</li><li>Performs complex testing procedures to isolate data communications transmission failures, utilizing data scopes, transmission test sets, data set testing procedures and time domain reflectometers; may assist the Technical Support Group in solving difficult/technical protocol and data transmission problems on an as needed basis;</li><li>Travels as necessary for education, meetings and site surveys for new terminal installations, relocations, and actual installation or relocation of terminals and minicomputers;</li><li>Communicates effectively, information to Property Control and Equipment Control Officer regarding installation, relocation, and/or removal of computer terminal equipment;</li><li>Performs other duties as required or assigned which are reasonably within the scope of the duties enumerated above.<br/><br/></li></ul><strong>Education and Work Experience:<br/><br/></strong>Requires knowledge, skill and mental development equivalent to completion of two (2) years of college, with coursework in the computer science field <strong>AND</strong> 4.5 years of technical systems, programming and/or IT project management experience, including at least 1-year supervisory experience or any equivalent combination of education and experience.<br/><br/><strong>Application Process: <br/><br/></strong><ul><li>Please visit to apply by completing the online application;
